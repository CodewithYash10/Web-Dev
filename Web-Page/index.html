The Rank of a Matrix

($\text{rank}(A)$) - Unlocking Matrix Properties

üéØ Definition

The **RANK** of a matrix $A$, denoted as $\text{rank}(A)$, is a single number that represents the **dimension of the vector space formed by its rows or columns.**

It is the **number of linearly independent rows or columns** in a matrix.
Key Fact: The number of independent rows and independent columns is always the same for any given matrix.

‚öôÔ∏è Method: Row Echelon Form (REF)

The most reliable way to find the rank is to convert the matrix to its Row Echelon Form (REF) using Gaussian Elimination.

$$ \text{rank}(A) = \text{Number of Non-Zero Rows in the Row Echelon Form of } A $$

Elementary Row Operations (E.R.O.s)

These operations are used to transform a matrix into REF without changing its rank:

  • **Swap:** Interchanging two rows ($R_i \leftrightarrow R_j$).
  • **Scale:** Multiplying a row by a non-zero scalar ($kR_i \to R_i$, where $k \neq 0$).
  • **Replace:** Adding a multiple of one row to another row ($R_i + kR_j \to R_i$).

üìù Example Calculation

Find the rank of the matrix $A$:

$$ A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ -1 & 0 & 3 & 5 \\ 1 & 4 & 5 & 5 \end{pmatrix} $$

Step 1: Eliminate entries below the first pivot (1).

$$ \begin{align*} R_2 &\leftarrow R_2 + R_1 \\ R_3 &\leftarrow R_3 - R_1 \end{align*} \implies \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 4 & 5 \\ 0 & 2 & 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix} $$

Step 2: Eliminate entries below the second pivot (2).

$$ R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - R_2 \implies \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 4 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0

Step 3: Count the non-zero rows.

The final matrix is in Row Echelon Form (REF).

Non-Zero Rows: $R_1$ and $R_2$. ($R_3$ is a zero row).

$$ \text{Number of Non-Zero Rows} = 2 $$

‚úÖ Calculation Result

The Rank of $A$ is:

$$ \boxed{\text{rank}(A) = 2} $$

üí° Uses of Rank

The rank of a matrix is a powerful concept with numerous applications in mathematics, science, and engineering:

üìà

System Solvability

Determining if a system of linear equations $Ax=b$ has a unique solution, infinite solutions, or no solution.

‚öôÔ∏è

Matrix Invertibility

A square matrix $A$ is invertible if and only if $\text{rank}(A)$ equals its number of rows (or columns).

üìä

Data Compression

Used in techniques like Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) for dimensionality reduction and image processing.

Machine Learning

Important in understanding the complexity of data, feature selection, and algorithm stability.

The rank of a matrix is a powerful indicator of its structural properties and the linear independence within its rows and columns.

Understanding rank is crucial for solving linear systems, analyzing data, and many advanced topics in science and engineering.
